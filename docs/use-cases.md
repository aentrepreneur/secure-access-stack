# Secure Access Stack Use Cases

## Primary Use Cases

| Use Case | Description | Primary Outcome |
|----------|-------------|-----------------|
| MFA for VPN | Strengthen remote access with token-based authentication | Reduced credential exposure |
| Admin portal hardening | Protect dashboards and back-office systems | Stronger privileged access control |
| Directory-backed auth | Centralize identity resolution via LDAP | Consistent policy across services |
| Compliance-oriented access | Introduce auditable identity controls | Better evidence posture |

## Customer Scenarios

### Small Business With Remote Staff

- strengthen VPN access for distributed teams
- reduce dependency on password-only authentication
- keep operational ownership with a local provider or internal team

### SMB With Internal Administrative Systems

- harden administrative dashboards and management interfaces
- centralize stronger authentication logic across services
- improve traceability and audit trails for privileged access

### Compliance-Sensitive Environment

- introduce stronger identity assurance for regulated systems
- make access controls easier to explain to auditors and stakeholders
- improve evidence posture for access-related reviews and assessments

### Managed Service Provider Scenario

- offer MFA and identity hardening as a service line to SMB clients
- maintain centralized operational visibility across deployments
- deliver audit-ready access evidence as a standard outcome

## Business Outcomes

- lower exposure to credential misuse and lateral movement
- lower operational friction compared to upstream identity tooling
- better audit posture with built-in evidence collection
- stronger identity control with regional operational ownership
- serviceable model instead of one-time technical install

## Why These Use Cases Matter

The differentiator is not only the MFA feature set. It is the combination of deployment speed, maintainability, local operational fit, and productized delivery that makes the stack practical for SMB environments that larger vendors do not effectively serve.
